# The One-Shot Model

## Why Legal Infrastructure is Not SaaS

---

## Thesis

Most digital legal solutions are built on a wrong premise: that value resides in continuous access to the system. The one-shot model starts from a different premise: value is not in access, value is in irreversible execution. This difference is not semantic; it is architectural, economic, and doctrinal.

---

## 1. The Error of the SaaS Model Applied to Law

The SaaS model was designed to retain users, build accounts, accumulate data, maximize time within the system, and monetize continuity. That model works perfectly for systems like CRM, project management, collaborative tools, and content platforms.

But a legal act is not continuous, it is punctual. A lawyer does not "use" a lawsuit, they file it. A professional does not "maintain" an answer, they execute it. Procedural law is not a subscription, it is a sequence of formal acts with consequences. Applying SaaS to litigation is forcing an economic structure foreign to the nature of the legal act.

---

## 2. The Legal Act as an Irreversible Event

In the one-shot model, the system does not offer permanent access. It offers an execution, a draft, a download, and subsequent total destruction. There is no history, library, projects, persistent versioning, or future resumption.

The system does not accumulate memory, does not build a relationship, and does not keep evidence. It operates under a simple principle: one execution produces an artifact, the artifact is downloaded, and everything else disappears.

---

## 3. From Platform to Machine

A SaaS is a platform. A platform has users, accounts, continuity, and retention as a central metric. The one-shot model is not a platform, it is a machine. A machine is activated, executes, produces, and turns off.

There is no "continuous use", there is punctual activation. The system does not seek for the professional to remain inside; it optimizes so they exit as soon as possible with a valid result. The metric is not time on site, it is time until download.

---

## 4. Payment as Authority, Not Access

In the SaaS model, you pay for access, for membership, and for continuity. In the one-shot model, you pay for execution authority.

The payment does not enable an account; it enables a single irreversible crossing. Once that authority is consumed, it cannot be reused, extended, or restarted. There are no credits, no balance, and no wallet. Each execution is an isolated economic event.

---

## 5. Deletion as a Trust Model

Traditional SaaS builds trust by promising storage security, recoverable history, backup, and access control. The one-shot model builds trust by absence: there is no storage, no history, no persistent data, no subsequent recovery.

Information is not kept "secure", it is destroyed. This is not a "feature", it is the core of the economic model. If there is no retained data, there is no future exploitation surface.

---

## 6. Strategic Consequence

The one-shot model implies that there is no retention as a KPI, no cumulative LTV per user, no lock-in, and no closed ecosystem.

Sustainability does not come from client dependence, it comes from the clarity of the act. Each execution is independent, each session ends in incineration, and each interaction is self-sufficient.

---

## 7. What This Model Is Not

It is not a legal suite, a workspace, a document manager, a continuous assistant, a collaborative environment, or a knowledge repository.

Any move toward those categories creates another different business. The one-shot model is deliberately restrictive. Restriction is its strength.

---

## 8. Architecture as Doctrine

This model is not a UX preference. It is an operational doctrine.

It defines how it is charged, how it is executed, what is allowed, what is prohibited, and what is destroyed. Architecture imposes limits that marketing cannot soften. Relationship is not sold, permanence is not sold, "use" is not sold. An act is sold.

---

## Conclusion

Procedural law is irreversible by nature and the one-shot model simply respects that structure. Where SaaS seeks permanence, the one-shot model seeks finality. Where SaaS accumulates, the one-shot model eliminates. Where SaaS retains, the one-shot model executes and disappears. It is not software that is used, it is an execution that is authorized. And it ends.

# Payment as Authority

## The Economics of Irreversibility

---

## Thesis

In most software, paying means gaining access.

In the one-shot model, paying means authorizing an irreversible act.

The difference is not commercial. It is structural.

> Payment does not buy use.
> Payment grants authority to execute a single transformation.

---

## 1. The Conceptual Error of "Access"

The traditional SaaS model assumes that you pay to enter, to remain, and to have the right to use the system. This generates accounts, credits, plans, subscriptions, and renewals.

The system is designed for continuity.

But a legal act is not continuous. It is discrete.

You don't need "permanent access" to an answer. You need to produce and file it.

---

## 2. The Rubicon: The Point of No Return

In the one-shot model, there is a single critical moment:

The crossing of irreversibility.

Before payment, the environment is reversible: it can be corrected, abandoned, and the final act has not been executed. After payment, the base information is frozen, authority is consumed, the draft is generated only once, and there is no restart.

Payment does not open a door; it activates a mechanism.

---

## 3. Authority, Not Entitlement

Payment does not create a permanent identity or a cumulative right.

It grants an execution, a result, and a closure. It does not grant a balance, a reusable account, a set of future rights, or a pending credit.

Each payment is autonomous, each execution is independent, and there is no economy of accumulation.

---

## 4. Fixed Cost Before Irreversibility

Price is not arbitrary or emotional.

It is determined: Before the crossing, based on structural document data, and in a deterministic way.

Once issued: The cost is frozen, it is not adjusted after execution, and it is not negotiated post-act.

This eliminates: Ambiguity, variable billing based on time, and dependence on hours.

The economy becomes transparent and atomic.

---

## 5. Exact Consumption of Authority

Authority is consumed exactly once.

If the execution: Finishes correctly → the document is downloaded. If it fails structurally → the session is incinerated.

It does not exist: Retries, re-executions, "try again at no cost," or session extensions.

The model is binary:

> It executes once.
> Or it does not execute.

In both cases, authority is consumed.

---

## 6. Economic Consequence

This model implies: No LTV per user, no financial lock-in, and no automatic recurring income.

Sustainability does not depend on forced retention.

It depends on: Value clarity, result precision, and trust in the act.

Each transaction must justify itself.

There is no cross-subsidy between sessions.

---

## 7. Elimination of Distorted Incentives

When income depends on subscriptions: Permanence, slight friction, and continuous use are incentivized.

When income depends on executions: Precision, speed, and clean closure are incentivized.

The metric is not time in the system. It is time until result.

---

## 8. What This Model Rejects

Payment as authority excludes: Memberships, prepaid credits, internal wallets, volume discounts that alter the nature of the act, and "professional plans."

If rights accumulation is introduced, the model ceases to be one-shot.

Economic purity is a condition for architectural coherence.

---

## Conclusion

In the SaaS model: Paying is gaining access.

In the one-shot model: Paying is crossing the Rubicon.

One does not acquire membership. One does not acquire continuity. One does not acquire future access.

A single irreversible act is authorized.

It is executed. The result is downloaded. Authority is consumed. And the infrastructure is emptied.
